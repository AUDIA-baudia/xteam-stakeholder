08 / Review System

Monthly Check-ins, Not Annual Surprises

Each month, the employee or commissioned individual brings a self-evaluation to the supervisor ‚Äî rating their progress toward total completion of each goal on a 1‚Äì10 scale.

The supervisor affirms or gently redirects, then asks: "How can I help you make progress this month?"

Key Insight: It's far more comfortable for a supervisor to gently correct a self-assessment monthly than to prepare once a year to address shortcomings. Most people naturally underestimate their progress ‚Äî so more often, the supervisor is lifting them up.

09 / Performance & Compensation

Performance Drives Value

Whether someone is hourly, salaried, or commissioned, their value in the marketplace is driven by the same fundamentals: skill set, experience, and wisdom. Increase your performance and you will increase your compensation over time.

90% ‚Üí 90%

Hourly & Salaried: The percentage of goal achievement is directly tied to the potential increase. Achieve 90% of your goals, receive 90% of the potential increase for that period.

Commissioned Individuals: Staying on track to hit goals and receiving coaching ensures that growing skill, experience, and collaboration translate into increased earning power in the marketplace over time. The system drives the same outcome through a different mechanism.

Market-Based Strategy
Decide on a percentile in the market as a macro-strategy, then use objective data from sources like Salary.com, PayScale, and Lattice.
Getting Back on Track
If someone falls short of 100%, supervisors are clear about what's expected. Extraordinary performance in the next segment gets them back on track.
The Bottom Line
The system gives people ownership of their career trajectory and value in the marketplace ‚Äî regardless of how they are compensated.
